BiCAPT: Bidirectional Computer-Assisted Pronunciation Training

An end-to-end style-preserving mispronunciation detection and correction system

Our system detects and corrects the mispronunciation speech simultaneously. What's more, we preserve the speech style in the correction process, which is natural and efficient under different CAPT requirements.

For our method, 'Monophone' preserves more style information, making the output more similar to the raw student input (and thus more natural). As the detection is not absolutely accurate, the detection results may contain false negative samples (i.e., the pronounciation that is supposed to be mispronounced but ignored by the system). We can choose to correct more phonemes to overcome this. 'Triphone' method sacrifices some style information to achieve better correction performance (lower WER). The results of these two versions both have high qualities and show a trade-off between style information and correction performance (as the performance of using detected labels is close to 'Triphone', for simplicity , we only show the 'Monophone' and 'Triphone' samples which use the annotated labels). Compared with other models, our method is more tiny and slim. 'Google TTS' cannot preserve raw pronunciation style. 'Glow TTS' can only maintain partial speaking style by combining the speaker embedding. 'Spectrum-based' methods will cause inconsistency and have a low quality of generation.
